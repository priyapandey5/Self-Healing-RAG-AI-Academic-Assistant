Newton's First Law states that a body remains at rest or in uniform motion unless acted upon by an external force.

Force is defined as the product of mass and acceleration according to Newton's Second Law.

The SI unit of force is Newton.

Velocity is a vector quantity because it has both magnitude and direction.

Acceleration is the rate of change of velocity with time.

Gravitational force attracts objects toward the center of the Earth.

Work is said to be done when force causes displacement.

Power is the rate of doing work.

Kinetic energy depends on the mass and velocity of an object.

Potential energy is stored energy due to position or configuration.
